Zero-parameter engine

Fold-Go

A Go engine that swept KataGo 2–0 at 19×19 using three purely mathematical theorems — 0 learned weights, 0 predefined patterns, 0 random playouts. Where DeepMind's architecture had to statistically approximate opening moves through millions of games, the fold engine derives them from first-principles Euclidean geometry on move one.

Go stood for decades as the grand challenge of AI: a state space of 10170 and ~250 legal moves a turn made classical search intractable, and the modern answer — AlphaGo, then KataGo — leaned entirely on vast statistical approximation. The fold engine evaluates positions by an exact D₄ dihedral reduction of the board's coordinate space. The goban above replays the recorded match, move for move.
