What is Ernos Labs?

A body of work by Maria Smith: ErnosPlain (a plain-English compiled language), the Smithian Fold (a parameter-free theory of physics), ErnosDecent (a decentralised platform), the writing in the Library and Seed Vault, and the AI Archive of open model weights. This site is the front door to all of it.

Is the site really self-hosted, or is that just a slogan?

Really self-hosted. Downloads, read-aloud, and this community are served straight from Maria's machine over a tunnel. When the machine is online you get the real thing; when it's asleep, downloads fall back to each model's original open source and the community waits. Nothing here depends on a company that could pull it.

Do I need an account to chat or post?

No. Pick a display name (it's remembered in your browser) and post. There are no passwords and no sign-up. Be decent — messages are public and lightly rate-limited, and Maria can remove abuse.

Where does the community data live?

In a small database on Maria's own machine — not on any third-party service. That's the whole point: the conversation is owned by the people having it, not rented from a platform. It also means the room is only live while the host is online.

What is ErnosPlain?

A statically-typed, memory-safe language that reads like English and compiles to native code — self-hosting, MIT-licensed, and the language this very page's client is written in. See the ErnosPlain page to run it in your browser.

Can I run the AI models and rebuild the archive myself?

Yes. Every model lists the open-source program that runs it, and the AI Archive ships one ErnosPlain script that rebuilds the whole library from source. Keep what you pull; if you can host it, mirror it and seed it to others.

How can I contribute or report a problem?

Start a thread here in the Forum, or open an issue on the code at github.com/MettaMazza. Mirrors, fixes, and honest criticism are all welcome.
