SLAB 3 — THE BODY
WHAT KILLS AND HOW TO STOP IT


PRIORITY ORDER

These kill fastest. Check and fix in this order:
1. AIRWAY — if they cannot breathe, nothing else matters. Clear the mouth. Tilt head back, lift chin.
2. BLEEDING — direct pressure with the cleanest cloth you have. Push hard. Do not release to check. If blood soaks through, add more cloth on top. If a limb wound will not stop and the person is dying, tie a strip tight above the wound to cut off blood flow. This may cost the limb. It saves the life.
THE FOLD: lose 1/3 of blood = shock. Lose 1/2 = death. These fractions are exact thresholds.
3. HEART STOPPED — lay the person flat on their back on a hard surface. Place the heel of your hand on the center of the chest. Push down hard, fast, steady — 100 pushes per minute, deep enough to compress the chest. Every 30 pushes, tilt the head back, seal your mouth over theirs, and blow two breaths. Continue until they respond or you cannot continue.
THE FOLD: compress 1/3 of chest depth. Ratio 30:2. Exact fractions govern the boundary of life.
4. SHOCK — the body shutting down from blood loss, pain, or trauma. Lay flat. Elevate legs. Keep warm. Give small sips of water if conscious.


WOUNDS

CLEAN: wash the wound with the cleanest water available. Boiled water is best. Remove dirt, debris, fragments.
CLOSE: for deep cuts, press the edges together. Hold with thin strips of clean cloth or adhesive.
COVER: wrap with clean cloth. Change the covering daily.
WATCH: redness spreading from the wound, heat, swelling, pus, red streaks moving toward the heart = infection. Clean again aggressively. Apply honey directly to the wound (honey kills many infectious organisms). If the infection spreads despite cleaning, the limb or the life may be lost.

CLEAN EVERYTHING. Boil any tool that touches a wound. Boil cloth for bandages. Wash your hands before touching any wound. Infection kills more wounded people than the wound itself.


BROKEN BONES

Signs: swelling, deformity, grinding sensation, inability to use the limb.
Treatment: ALIGN the bone ends as close to natural position as possible (pull firmly and steadily — this is painful). SPLINT — bind rigid material (straight sticks, bark, boards) on both sides of the break, extending past the joint above and below. IMMOBILIZE — the broken part must not move while it heals. Wait 6-8 weeks for major bones.

If bone pierces the skin (open fracture): treat the wound first (clean, cover), then splint. High infection risk.


BURNS

COOL immediately with clean water. Hold under running water or submerge for as long as the person can stand.
Do NOT apply grease, butter, oil, or anything except water and clean covering.
Do NOT break blisters — they protect the tissue underneath.
Cover loosely with clean cloth. Watch for infection.
Severe burns (large area, charred skin) = life-threatening. Keep the person warm, hydrated, and as clean as possible.


CHOKING

If someone cannot breathe, cough, or speak:
Stand behind them. Wrap your arms around their waist. Make a fist. Place it above the navel, below the ribs. Pull sharply inward and upward. Repeat until the obstruction clears or the person loses consciousness.

If unconscious: lay flat, attempt to remove the obstruction by hand if visible, begin chest compressions.


REHYDRATION

Dehydration kills. The treatment is simple:
In clean water, dissolve a pinch of salt and a larger amount of sugar (six parts sugar to one part salt). Drink in small, frequent sips. This replaces what the body loses through vomiting, diarrhea, or sweating. It has saved more lives than any medicine.
THE FOLD: 6:1 sugar to salt. Salt = 1/7 of the total solute. An exact fraction.


CHILDBIRTH

Most births proceed without intervention. Your job is to provide CLEAN conditions.
- Clean hands, clean surface, clean cloths.
- The mother's body knows what to do. Do not rush the process.
- After the baby emerges, clear the mouth and nose. The baby should cry and breathe.
- Cut the cord only after it stops pulsing. Tie it tightly in two places. Cut between the ties with a clean blade.
- Deliver the afterbirth (it follows within an hour). If it does not come, this is dangerous.
- Watch for excessive bleeding after delivery. This is the greatest risk.

INTERVENE ONLY IF: the baby is stuck (breech — feet first, or shoulder caught), bleeding is severe, or the cord is around the neck. These are life-threatening emergencies.


DROWNING

Do NOT approach a drowning person from the front — they will grab you and pull you under.
Approach from behind. Grab hair, collar, or arms. Tow to safety.
If you cannot reach them: throw rope, a log, anything that floats.

Once out: clear the airway, tilt the head, give rescue breaths, begin compressions if no heartbeat. Watch for hours afterward — water in the lungs can cause delayed breathing failure.


POISONING

If swallowed and NOT caustic (does not burn the mouth): induce vomiting. Finger to back of throat.
If caustic (burns the mouth/lips): do NOT induce vomiting. Dilute with water or milk.
Crushed charcoal mixed with water absorbs many poisons. Have the person drink it.
If unconscious, lay on their side so vomit does not block the airway.


SNAKE AND INSECT BITES

Keep the person STILL and CALM. Movement spreads venom.
Immobilise the bitten limb. Keep it at or below heart level.
Do NOT cut the bite. Do NOT suck the venom. Do NOT tourniquet.
Watch for: rapid spreading swelling, difficulty breathing, vision changes, uncontrolled bleeding. These signs = dangerous venom.
Many bites are survivable with rest and time. The body processes small doses of many venoms.


PAIN AND FEVER

WILLOW BARK TEA: strip inner bark from young willow branches. Chop. Steep in hot water. Drink. Reduces pain and fever. Takes effect within an hour. Bitter. Works.

CLOVE: crush a dried clove. Press against an aching tooth and gum. Numbs the area within minutes.

POPPY: the sap of the unripe seed pod is the most powerful natural pain reliever. It is also LETHAL in slight excess and DESTROYS through addiction. Use ONLY for severe acute pain — surgery, crushed limbs, dying patients. NEVER for ongoing pain. Guard access to it. The difference between a healing dose and a killing dose is small.


THE BODY'S SIGNALS

Swelling = infection or injury
Pale skin = blood loss
Yellow eyes = liver failure
Dark urine = dehydration
Rapid breathing = shock or panic
High fever = the body fighting infection

Listen to the body. It does not lie.
