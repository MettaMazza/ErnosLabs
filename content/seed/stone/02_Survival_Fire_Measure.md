DAY-ONE SURVIVAL


MEASURING WITH YOUR BODY

FINGER WIDTH — the smallest unit
HAND SPAN — thumb tip to little finger tip, spread wide
FOREARM — elbow to fingertip
ARM SPAN — fingertip to fingertip, arms stretched wide
PACE — one full step
HEIGHT — top of head to ground

To make a standard: pick one person. Mark their measurements on a straight stick. Copy that stick. Every copy is the same. Now everyone measures the same.
THE FOLD: every measurement is a ratio — one length against another. No ratio, no measure.

RIGHT ANGLES — THE 3-4-5 METHOD

Take a rope. Tie 12 equally spaced knots. Stretch it into a triangle: 3 spaces on one side, 4 on the next, 5 on the last. The corner between 3 and 4 is a perfect right angle. Every time. Verify by measuring — the two shorter sides' squares add to the longest side's square. 9 + 16 = 25.
THE FOLD: 3-4-5 is forced — the only triangle where whole-number squares sum exactly.


DIRECTION — THE SHADOW STICK

Plant a straight stick upright in open ground. The shadow moves as the sun moves.
- The SHORTEST shadow = midday = the direction of the sun's highest point.
- In the northern half of the world, shortest shadow points NORTH.
- In the southern half, it points SOUTH.

To find east-west: mark the shadow tip in the morning. Wait until afternoon when the shadow is the same length. Mark again. A straight line between the two marks runs east-west.


TIME

One full sun cycle = one day.
Moon: new to full and back = approximately 29.5 days.
Seasons: shadow length at midday changes through the year. Longest shadow = winter. Shortest shadow = summer.
Year: count full moon cycles between two matching shadow days = approximately 12.4 moon cycles.

WATER CLOCK: poke a small hole in a vessel. Fill with water. It drips at a constant rate. Mark the water level at equal time intervals. Consistent. Portable. Works in the dark.


ANGLES

A full turn = 360 degrees. A right angle = 90. A straight line = 180.
Your fist held at arm's length covers approximately 10 degrees of sky.
A spread hand covers approximately 20 degrees.
One finger width = approximately 2 degrees.
The angle of the pole star above the horizon = your latitude (your position between equator and pole).


TEMPERATURE

Water freezes = cold anchor.
Water boils = hot anchor.
These two points never change at the same altitude. Everything between can be divided into equal steps.


HEAT TRANSFER

CONDUCTION: heat moves through solid material. Touch a metal rod in a fire — the far end gets hot.
CONVECTION: hot air or liquid rises, cool falls. This is why smoke goes up and fire heats a room from the ceiling down.
RADIATION: heat travels as invisible energy through empty space. You feel the sun's heat. That heat crossed empty space to reach you.


STEAM POWER

Heat water in a sealed vessel. The steam expands and pushes. Direct that push against a piston (a plug that slides inside a tube). The piston moves. Connect the piston to a wheel. The wheel turns. Continuous heat = continuous rotation = mechanical work.


WATER WHEEL

Falling water hits paddles mounted on a wheel. The wheel turns. Connect the wheel's axle to any tool — a grinding stone, a hammer, a saw, a bellows. The river does the work.


WIND

Mount angled blades on an axle. Wind pushes the blades. The axle turns. Same principle as the water wheel, different power source.


MAGNETISM

A lodestone (a natural iron-bearing rock) pulls iron toward itself. Rub an iron needle on a lodestone in one direction, many times. The needle becomes magnetic. Float the needle on a leaf on still water, or suspend it from a thread. It points north. This is a compass. It works in fog, in clouds, at night, at sea.


ELECTRICITY

Two different metals (copper and zinc work well) placed in an acid liquid (vinegar, lemon juice) produce a continuous flow of invisible energy through a wire connecting them. This is a battery. Multiple batteries connected together produce stronger flow.

The flow through a wire:
- Produces heat (a thin wire glows — this is a lamp)
- Produces magnetism (wrap wire around an iron nail — it becomes a magnet while current flows)
- Produces motion (a wire carrying current near a magnet is pushed. This is a motor.)
- Can be produced by motion (spin a magnet inside a coil of wire — current flows. This is a generator.)

GENERATOR + MOTOR: they are the same device run in opposite directions. Motion in = electricity out. Electricity in = motion out.


MECHANICAL ADVANTAGE

LEVER: a rigid bar on a pivot (fulcrum). Move the fulcrum closer to the load, push on the long end. A small force moves a heavy load.

PULLEY: a wheel with a groove. Thread a rope through. One pulley changes direction of force (pull down to lift up). Two pulleys = half the effort for the same lift. More pulleys = more advantage.
THE FOLD: each added pulley halves the force — 1/2, 1/4, 1/8. These are dyadic rationals, the fold's own fractions.

INCLINED PLANE (ramp): pushing a load up a gentle slope requires less force than lifting it straight up. Longer ramp = less force needed.

SCREW: an inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder. Turns rotary motion into linear motion. Each turn advances a small distance with great force.

GEAR: two toothed wheels interlocked. A small gear driving a large one = slow speed, high force. A large gear driving a small one = high speed, low force. This is how you match a power source to a task.
THE FOLD: every gear ratio is an exact fraction. No approximation — teeth must mesh.
