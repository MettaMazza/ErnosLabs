SLAB 4 — THE SOIL
HOW TO GROW FOOD


THE SEED CYCLE

All food begins with a seed.
1. Put seed in prepared soil.
2. Add water. Add sunlight.
3. The plant grows, flowers, produces fruit or grain.
4. Inside the fruit or grain are new seeds.
5. Save seeds from your STRONGEST, HEALTHIEST plants.
6. Dry the seeds. Store in cool, dry, dark conditions.
7. Next season, plant them. Repeat forever.

SEED LIFE:
Onion and parsley seeds last 1-2 years.
Bean, pea, carrot seeds last 3-4 years.
Squash and cabbage seeds last 4-5 years.
Cucumber, melon, tomato seeds last 5+ years.
Wheat and rice grains last 5-10+ years if kept dry.

TEST BEFORE PLANTING: lay 10 seeds on damp cloth. Keep moist. If fewer than 6 sprout, the batch is weak — plant thickly or replace.
THE FOLD: 6 of 10 = 3/5. A rational threshold — the fraction decides viability.


SOIL

Good soil is dark, crumbly, damp, full of worms and roots. Bad soil is pale, powdery, or dense clay.

COMPOSTING — HOW TO BUILD SOIL:
1. Pile plant waste, food scraps (no meat), and animal manure in a heap.
2. Keep the heap damp. Turn it with a fork every few weeks to let air in.
3. In weeks to months it becomes dark, crumbly material that smells like earth.
4. Mix this into your growing soil. It feeds plants.

What goes wrong: soil that grows the same crop every year becomes exhausted and diseased.


CROP ROTATION

Never plant the same crop in the same ground two years in a row. Rotate different plants through different areas each season. This breaks disease cycles and lets the soil recover what each crop takes.
THE FOLD: rotation is a periodic cycle — the pattern returns to its start, like the fold's own period.

A simple rotation: heavy feeders (corn, squash) one year, soil builders (beans, clover) the next, root crops (carrots, turnips) the third, then rest or compost.


THE THREE SISTERS

Corn + Beans + Squash planted together in the same ground.
- CORN grows tall. Beans climb it.
- BEANS fix nutrients in the soil that corn needs.
- SQUASH spreads on the ground, shading soil — retaining moisture, suppressing weeds.

Together they produce more food than any of them alone. They also provide a complete diet: grain, protein, vitamins.


COMPANION PLANTING

GARLIC and ONIONS near vegetables repel many insect pests.
MARIGOLDS around beds repel soil-dwelling pests.
SUNFLOWERS attract pollinators — plant near crops that need pollination.

WALNUT TREES poison the soil around them. Do not plant food gardens near walnuts.
POTATOES and TOMATOES planted together share diseases. Keep apart.


FOOD PRESERVATION

Food rots. These methods stop or slow rot:

DRYING: slice thin. Lay in sun and moving air, or hang over low smoke. Dried food lasts months to years.
SMOKING: expose to continuous wood smoke for hours or days. The smoke deposits preserving compounds. Meat and fish last months.
SALTING: pack food in salt or soak in heavy salt water. Salt draws moisture out and prevents the organisms that cause rot.
FERMENTATION: submerge vegetables in salt water in a sealed vessel. Beneficial organisms consume sugars and produce acid. The acid prevents rot. Lasts months. Also produces vitamins the raw food did not have.
COOL STORAGE: an underground pit or cellar stays cool year-round. Root vegetables, apples, and sealed preserves last through winter in a cool, dark, dry pit.


WATER

Plants need water. Rules:
- Water the roots, not the leaves. Wet leaves invite disease.
- Water in the morning. Evening watering leaves moisture overnight = rot and fungus.
- Deep, infrequent watering is better than shallow, frequent watering. It encourages deep roots.
- Mulch (dead leaves, straw, wood chips) spread around plants reduces water loss from the soil surface.


ANIMALS

Domesticated animals provide meat, milk, eggs, leather, wool, bone, fat, and labor.
FENCING: an animal you cannot contain is an animal you do not have. Fence first.
FEEDING: animals need water daily and food appropriate to their type. Malnourished animals do not produce.
BREEDING: mate your strongest, healthiest animals. Over generations, your stock improves. This is directly observable.
SLAUGHTER: kill quickly and cleanly. Bleed the carcass completely. Butcher immediately. Preserve what you cannot eat within a day.


DANGEROUS FUNGI

NEVER eat a mushroom or fungus you cannot positively identify.
Some edible and deadly fungi look nearly identical.
When in doubt, do not eat.

Signs of the most dangerous type: white gills, a ring around the stem, a cup-like structure at the base. Causes liver failure. Kills in days. No cure.
